The Opportunity in One Sentence

I lead a small team that builds "jigs"—specialized tooling and processes that solve Commerce.com's most complex customer migration and integration problems once, then hands them to Core Product teams as repeatable platform capabilities.

The Pitch

Instance 001: WP Engine partnership + WooCommerce migration. 8,000 mid-market stores need migration path. We've proven technical feasibility with working prototypes. Next: First 10 customer pilots to validate time-to-first-value and unit economics.

The Ask: Stage 2 Gate Decision

Should We Progress to First 10 Pilots?
We've proven technical feasibility (Stage 1). The next gate requires executive commitment to Tiger Team formation and WP Engine partnership activation.

Decision Criteria: Is WooCommerce migration a strategic priority? Can we secure 10 pilot customers from WP Engine? Are we willing to operate a 3-person Tiger Team for 6 months to validate?

Risk Mitigation

What if this doesn't work? The operating model has built-in kill gates. If First 10 pilots fail (TTFV >3 weeks, NPS <40, too many support issues), we kill the initiative at Month 5. Total investment: 3 people × 5 months = 15 person-months. Traditional approach would have spent 4-5 weeks × 4 people = 20 person-weeks before discovering the same issues.

What if resource lending breaks down? The model only works if VPs honor Resource Lease Agreements. If VPs systematically revoke engineers mid-project, operating model fails. CFO validation suggests ultra-small team (3 people) can operate in "stealth mode" initially, minimizing political resistance.

What if Path 2 doesn't scale? The POC validates Path 2 (headless) as technically viable, but we don't know if customers want it. First 10 pilots will tell us. If Path 2 adoption is weak, we pivot to Path 1 (full migration) or Path 3 (hybrid/phased) based on customer feedback.
