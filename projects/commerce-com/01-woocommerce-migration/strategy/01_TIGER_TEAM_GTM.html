Understanding the opportunity gap in the WooCommerce migration market

Strategic Context

The Opportunity Gap

48% of platform migrations default to Shopify

Not because Shopify is always better—but because it's the path of least resistance. A meaningful segment (20-30% per internal hypothesis) may be better suited to the Commerce.com Stack, but merchants don't know it's an option. Source: Cart2Cart 2024

The Critical Insight

WooCommerce merchants aren't leaving WordPress because they hate WordPress. They're leaving because WooCommerce (the commerce plugin) can't scale.

  • WordPress CMS = Content management (excellent, SEO-rich)
  • WooCommerce = Commerce engine (the failing component)

The WPEngine Problem

Every WooCommerce migration represents:

  • $500-$2,000/month lost hosting revenue
  • 5-7 year customer relationship ending
  • "WordPress can't do commerce" narrative

Option 3 is the only viable retention strategy—and Commerce.com is the ideal partner.

Commerce.com Tiger Team 05
Approach

Why Stealth Mode?

Stealth Rationale

  • Competitive intelligence risk: Public announcement alerts Shopify
  • Partnership complexity: WPEngine may have Shopify relationships
  • Narrative control: Need proof before claims
  • Execution validation: Must work before scaling

Stealth Criteria

Do Don't
Build integration quietly Issue press releases
Pilot with NDA merchants Publish case studies (yet)
Use partner channels Announce at conferences
Validate metrics internally Over-promise before proof

Trigger for public announcement: 3+ successful pilot migrations, quantified metrics, WPEngine formal partnership agreement, case study permissions
